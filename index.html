Stanford-related

I am often asked, "which classes should I take at Stanford"/"how to make the most of my time at Stanford" by aspiring undergraduates. Here's the short take.
  • Cornerstones. Figure out the top five classes which interest you, and solve the dependency graph so that you get to take them all before you graduate. Here were mine:
    • CS 155, EE 364A/B, MATH 145, CS 231N, CS 261.
  • Other classes which I think are a worthwhile addition to any undergraduate curriculum regardless of major.
    • ARTHIST 1B with Alex Nemerov.
    • Social I with Richard Powers.
    • English 167H, The Ethical Gangster, with Blakey Vermeule.
    • I've heard good things about Improv.
    • Take EMED 110, tell all your friends to take it, save a life.
  • Classes I wish I'd taken.
    • Decision Making Under Uncertainty (CS 238).
